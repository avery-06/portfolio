Project 04

Ball Bearing & Screwdriver Dissections

CourseMIE 243 — Mechanical Engineering Design

Overview

For MIE 243, my team and I were tasked with dissecting a ball bearing and a screwdriver, then recreating precise engineering drawings and 3D CAD models. Both tasks required careful caliper measurements and a solid understanding of mechanical features to accurately reverse-engineer each component.

Ball Bearing Dissection

  • Measured inner and outer race diameters, ball diameter, and groove radii using digital calipers.
  • Produced concentric engineering drawings capturing all critical dimensions including R values for the races.

Screwdriver Dissection & Modification

  • Created a full SolidWorks 3D model of the original screwdriver based on caliper measurements.
  • Designed a novel modification: a push-button shaft-lock mechanism that prevents the shaft from rotating when engaged.
  • Required careful consideration of fits, tolerances, and the actuation geometry to ensure reliable engagement and disengagement.
