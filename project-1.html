Project 01

Cinematography Robotic Arm

CourseMIE 243 — Mechanical Engineering Design
RoleCAD Lead · Motor Selection
Tags
CADAssembly and Exploded ViewsBOMIterative Design

Overview

For my design course MIE 243, my team and I were tasked with creating an advanced conceptual design of a low-cost, long-reach robot arm targeting advanced hobbyists and beginner cinematographers. I primarily worked on 3D modelling the robot in SolidWorks and selecting motors based on torque and speed requirements.

Design

  • The robot arm sits on a motorized dolly and has six degrees of freedom (DOF), enabling flexible camera positioning.
  • I created an exploded view assembly drawing with a full Bill of Materials (BOM) for a key sub-assembly.
  • Detailed concentric engineering drawings were produced for non-standardized parts.

Iterations

  • The team initially designed the dolly with a belt drive system but switched to a motorized wheel drive after determining the belt drive was too costly for the project budget.
  • Multiple candidate designs were evaluated at each stage before converging on the final configuration.

Key Takeaways

This project strengthened my SolidWorks skills — from part modelling to assembly management and engineering drawings. It also gave me hands-on experience with motor sizing and the importance of cost-driven design decisions in iterative engineering.
